# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Design-aware visitors who arrive with high expectations: creative directors, senior recruiters, potential agency clients, peer designers and developers. They scan fast, reward craft, and immediately sense when something is templated. They visit on desktop primarily; a recruiter might pull it up on their phone mid-meeting. They are not looking to be onboarded — they want to be convinced.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Yunus Emre Korkmaz, UI/UX designer and frontend developer. The site exists to prove that Yunus thinks in systems, executes with precision, and has a point of view worth hiring. Success = a visitor who reaches out or recommends the site to someone else.

## Brand Personality

Intentional. Quiet. Precise. Not minimal by laziness — minimal by conviction. The bracket notation ([yeqq], [principles]) and all-lowercase copy signal a voice that has made deliberate choices. The personality is a craftsperson who sweats the invisible details.

## Anti-references

Generic "creative developer" portfolios that lead with a big bold name, a particle background, and a 4-column case-study grid. Sites that use Inter or Outfit because it felt safe. Portfolios that look like they were generated from a template and tweaked. Awwwards nominees that follow the same editorial-typographic lane (display serif + mono labels + ruled separators).

## Design Principles

1. **Every choice is a statement.** Nothing on this site should be there by default. Fonts, spacing, motion, copy — all should have a reason.
2. **Restraint is not emptiness.** The monochrome system earns its confidence only when the moments of contrast (color, scale, motion) land with precision.
3. **Show, don't explain.** The site itself is the portfolio. Code quality, animation craft, and interaction detail do the talking.
4. **Voice over template.** The bracket notation, lowercase everything, and terse philosophy copy are identity markers. They should deepen, not dilute.
5. **The visitor decides in 3 seconds.** The above-the-fold moment must answer "who is this and why does it matter" without requiring a scroll.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion respected (already implemented via `prefers-reduced-motion`). Keyboard navigation for island navbar (ESC, Tab trap). Turkish + English i18n already in place.
